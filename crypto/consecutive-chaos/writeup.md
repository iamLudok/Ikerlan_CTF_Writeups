# Consecutive Chaos — Crypto | 300 pts | Medium

## Description

> Oops! It seems someone was in a hurry when generating RSA keys. You found a server that uses a pair of consecutive primes (p, q) to generate its modulus N. You have the modulus N and the public exponent e. Your mission is to intercept the encrypted message c and decrypt it to obtain the flag.

## Hints

> **Hint 1 (free):** When two numbers, p and q, are nearly equal, their product N=p·q approximates a perfect square. What mathematical operation can you perform on N to get an excellent estimate of p or q?

> **Hint 2 (free):** If p is the estimate closest to √N and p and q are very close, it means p and q are in a very narrow range around √N. Try brute-forcing or a simple search on the integers immediately before and after round(√N) until you find a divisor of N.
