# El Email Sospechoso — Forensics | 200 pts | Easy

## Description

> The IT department of IK3RL4N intercepted a phishing email that reached the inbox of several employees. At first glance, it looks like typical spam promising prizes and offers too good to be true. However, this email caught attention because several employees reported strange behaviour on their computers after "simply reading it". The incident response team suspects this is no ordinary phishing attempt. Modern attackers often hide additional payloads, hidden commands, or reconnaissance information inside the emails themselves, using techniques that go beyond simply "clicking the malicious link". Your job as a forensic analyst is to completely dissect this email and discover what additional information it contains. Sophisticated cybercriminals use every part of the email protocol for their malicious purposes.

## Hints

> **Hint 1 (free):** An email is like a physical envelope: what you see is only the content, but there is a lot of information on the envelope itself. Forensic investigators always examine the entire 'wrapper' of the message.

> **Hint 2 (free):** Email protocols allow custom headers that start with 'X-'. Attackers sometimes abuse this to hide data where normal users never look.

> **Hint 3 (50 pts):** Not used.
